The Age of Connectivity

Anyone Can
Broadcast Now

The 2000s marked a seismic shift from physical to digital. DVDs gave way to downloads, then to streaming. YouTube, Netflix, and Twitch redefined how people consumed entertainment — instantly, anywhere, on any device.

"The internet is the largest experiment in anarchy that we have ever had." — Eric Schmidt

Social media blurred the line between creator and audience. Anyone could now broadcast to millions. The digital era democratised creativity — giving rise to influencers, indie studios, podcasters, and interactive storytelling formats that no network executive could have imagined.

  • OTT Netflix turned binge-watching into a cultural norm
  • VID YouTube created the first generation of self-made media stars
  • LIVE Twitch made watching others play games a mainstream entertainment category
  • AUD Spotify rewrote the economics of the entire music industry
