The Beginning

Before Screens,
There Was Story

Long before screens and speakers, people found joy through performance, song, and narrative. Ancient Greek theatre, Roman amphitheatres, and Indian dance dramas turned creativity into shared cultural experience — teaching values, celebrating gods, and binding communities.

"Art is not what you see, but what you make others see." — Edgar Degas

Music and poetry were the original entertainment technology — using rhythm and melody to carry stories across generations without a single word written down. From the lyre to the drum, early entertainment was always a community affair.

  • DRA Greek and Roman theatre shaped the foundations of drama and comedy
  • MUS Music and rhythm carried culture across continents and centuries
  • ORL Oral storytelling preserved history, myth, and moral wisdom

Origins

Forms of Early Expression

Performance

Greek Theatre

From the 5th century BC, open-air amphitheatres hosted tragedies and comedies that explored the full range of human emotion. Masks, chorus, and choreography created a total theatrical experience.

Music

Ancient Instruments

The world's earliest playable instruments — bone flutes dating back 9,000 years — reveal that music predates writing itself. Harps, lyres, and drums were central to ritual and celebration across every culture.

Storytelling

Oral Traditions

Before literacy, professional storytellers preserved history, law, and myth through memorised narratives. Medieval bards and griots carried entire cultural libraries in their minds — passed on through apprenticeship.

Live Performance

Travelling Performers

Jesters, acrobats, puppeteers, and travelling troupes brought entertainment to towns and courts across Europe and Asia. Shakespeare's Globe Theatre emerged from this rich live-performance tradition.
