The Era of Broadcast

Culture Delivered
Into Every Home

The 1950s marked the rise of television as a household staple. Families gathered nightly around the glow of a cathode ray tube — watching variety shows, news bulletins, and sitcoms that became shared national memories.

"Television has made dictatorship impossible, but democracy unbearable." — Shimon Peres

Networks became the new stage. Iconic moments — the moon landing, Kennedy's assassination, Mandela walking free — demonstrated television's unique power to unite humanity in a single, simultaneous emotional experience.

  • CRT Black-and-white broadcasts evolved into vibrant colour by the 1960s
  • SAT Satellite TV connected global audiences to live events worldwide
  • CBL Cable channels fractured mass audiences into niche communities
